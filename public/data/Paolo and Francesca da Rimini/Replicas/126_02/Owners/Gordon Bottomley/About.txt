Gordon Bottomley (1874–1948) was an English poet, known for verse dramas. His main influences were the later Victorian Romantic poets, the Pre-Raphaelites and William Morris. Bottomley was a collector. In the 1940s Bottomley and his wife Emily gave their personal art collection of six hundred paintings, prints and drawings to the Tullie House Museum and Art Gallery in Carlisle, Cumberland, including works by Dante Gabriel Rossetti, Edward Burne-Jones, William Morris, Arthur Hughes, Ford Madox Brown, Elizabeth Siddal and Simeon Solomon, Stanley Spencer, Samuel Palmer, Albert Moore, Frederic Leighton, Henri Fantin-Latour, Lucien Pissarro, William Nicholson, Walter Crane, Charles Conder, Jessie Marion King, May Morris, William Rothenstein, Charles Ricketts and Paul Nash.
