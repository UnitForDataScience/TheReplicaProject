Frederick Craven (1818-1894): Calico printer, Bayley and Craven, Manchester; trustee of Cross Street Chapel and commissioner of the Peace for Derbyshire. Married Frances Brooke, had two children. Loaned works to the Manchester Art Treasure exhibition, 1857, Leeds in 1868, Manchester Jubilee, 1887, and Guildhall exhibition, 1894.  Collected watercolors by Aestheticism artists, Simeon Solomon and Edward Burne-Jones, and early English watercolors by Cox, Cattermole, De Wint, Holland, Henry Hunt, Prout, and Turner, He commissioned works directly form artists and bought from Agnew’s.  A patron of Ford Madox Brown.
