William Michael Rossetti  (1829-1919) was the brother of Maria Francesca Rossetti, artist and poet Dante Gabriel Rossetti and poet Christina Georgina Rossetti. He was one of the seven founder members of the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood in 1848, edited the Brotherhood's literary magazine The Germ (four issues in 1850) and wrote the poetry reviews for it. recorded the aims of the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood at their founding meeting in September 1848 and their history from then on. Rossetti worked full-time as a civil servant and still maintained a prolific output of criticism and biography in literature and art. He edited the diaries of his maternal uncle John William Polidori (author of The Vampyre and physician to Lord Byron), a comprehensive biography of D. G. Rossetti, and edited collected works of D. G. Rossetti and Christina Rossetti.  He was art critic for The Spectator from 1850.
