Robert Hitchins Camden Harrison (1837-1924), a partner, from 1861-1912, in the firm of London stockbrokers, Hitchins, Harrison and Co., was a stockbroker and musical connoisseur, who commissioned a famous country house, Shiplake Court, Henley built by Ernest George and Peto in 1889-90.  Active patron of John Singer Sargent, he was part of the high society of late Victorian Britain. In 1886, he bought Whistler’s Nocturne in Blue and Gold: Old Battersea Bridge.  He was also High Sheriff in 1900.  Sargent painted a famous portrait of Harrison’s wife Helen in 1886 (Tate).
