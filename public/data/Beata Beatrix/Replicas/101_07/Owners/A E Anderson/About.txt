Arthur E. Anderson (1870-1938). Brightling Hall, Robertsbridge Park Farm Cottage, Chessington, Surrey. Lived in a cottage in Chessington, and used his inherited money to buy works of art that he presented to institutions throughout the country, many of which he had never visited; work were often sent directly to them from dealers. Ruined in the Great Crash. Presented drawings to the British Museum.  He was an important benefactor of the Whitworth Museum in Manchester to which he donated 288 paintings, 5 sculptures, and 100 prints, including works by Rossetti.
