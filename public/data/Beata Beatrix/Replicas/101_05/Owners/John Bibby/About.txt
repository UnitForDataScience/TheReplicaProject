John Bibby (1814-92), Liverpool ship owner and copper manufacturer.  He and his brother in the family business oversaw the transition from sail to steamships. In 1873 they sold their interest to their junior partner Frederick Leyland, also a prominent art collector. They focused on their copper interests, then reentered shipping in 1889. Bibby was also director of the North and South Wales Bank.  He owned 9 Rossetti paintings and Arthur Hughes’s Ophelia, watercolors by Ford Madox Brown, oils by William Windus and Henry Wallis and works by earlier landscape painters Turner, Cox, De Wint, and Landseer.  He is NOT identical to John Bibby, collector, whose collection was sold in 1883.  DSM, 393.
