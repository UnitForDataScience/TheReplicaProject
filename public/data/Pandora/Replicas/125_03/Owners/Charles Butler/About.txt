Charles Butler of Hertford (1821-1910) was a prominent figure in the literary and art, as well as social circles of the Hertfordshire. Butler was educated privately in England and subsequently on the Continent. Attached to reading he studied ancient and modern literature. Butler was a Justice of the Peace and a Deputy Lieutenant of Hertfordshire, and High Sheriff of the County. He married in 1849 Miss Sarah Sophia Walker. Politically Mr. Butler is a Liberal-Unionist, and has always been a Free Trader and enemy of Protection in any form. When in London, Mr. Butler resided at his town house, 3, Connaught Place, W.
