James Dyson Perrins (circa 1824) at Worcester, the son of William Henry Perrins and Sarah Dyson. He married Frances Sarah Perrins, daughter of James Perrins and Lydia Grigg, in 1856 at Clerkenwell, London, England. He held the office of Justice of the Peace (J.P.). He was the owner of the Lea & Perrins Worcestershire sauce factory and the grandson of William Perrins, co-originator of the Lea & Perrins secret recipe. He was a director of Royal Worcester Porcelain Factory.
