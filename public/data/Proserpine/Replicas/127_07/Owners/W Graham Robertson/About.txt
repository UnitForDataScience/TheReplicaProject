Walford Graham Robertson (1866–1948) was a British painter, illustrator and author. He donated over 20 works of art to the Tate Gallery, London. Museums of England owe a debt to his taste and judgment. He was an important collector of art, especially of William Blake. He wrote plays, novels and a memoir, Time Was. He painted and illustrated books. He designed costumes for Sarah Bernhardt and Ellen Terry. He was twice portrayed by John Singer Sargent. He knew many notable people including Oscar Wilde and artists. The New York Times printed a biographical article about him in 2001.
