Lt-Col James Bellhouse Gaskell (1848-1925) of Roseleigh, Woolton, Liverpool, attended Owens College, Manchester, and worked with his two brothers in his father’s firm, Gaskell, Deacon and Co. a successfully Leblanc factory in Widnes. He was Justice of the Peace for Lancashire; director of the United Alkali Co., and of the White Moss Coal Co.; Maj. and Hon Lt Col, 3rd battalion South Lancashire Regent, for 21 years; member of the Council of the Liverpool Convalescent Home, Woolton. He traveled widely, especially to Japan and was a member of the Japan Society, London. He chaired the Woolton Liberal Unionist Association. He collected art and objets d’art and owned clocks later donated to the British Museum.
